# Update on McCamish Cyber Incident

In continuation to the statement dated March 14, 2025, Infosys updated that on August 5, 2025, Infosys McCamish Systems (“IMS”), a subsidiary of Infosys BPM Limited, which is a wholly owned subsidiary of Infosys Limited, and the State of Oregon Attorney General's Office entered into an Assurance of Voluntary Compliance (“AVC”).

The Oregon Attorney General's Office expressed concerns that IMS may have violated the Oregon Consumer Information Protection Act and the Oregon Unlawful Trade Practices Act with respect to a 2023 data breach.

IMS entered into the Assurance of Voluntary Compliance to resolve this matter without any further action. The AVC specifically acknowledges that this is not an admission or finding that IMS violated the Acts listed above, and that IMS does not admit any of the factual assertions contained in the AVC.

The details required under Regulation 30 of the SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015, as amended, are as below:

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| Name of the authority | State of Oregon (United States of America) Attorney General's Office |
| Nature and details of the action taken, initiated, or order passed | Assurance of Voluntary Compliance resolving the matter with no admission or finding that IMS violated the Oregon Consumer Information Protection Act or the Oregon Unlawful Trade Practices Act. |
| Date of receipt of direction or order, including any ad-interim or interim orders, or any other communication from the authority | August 5, 2025 |
| Details of the violations or contraventions committed or alleged to be committed | The Oregon Attorney General's Office expressed concerns regarding potential violations of the Oregon Consumer Information Protection Act and Oregon Unlawful Trade Practices Act, on the grounds that IMS failed to provide sufficient notice as required by the Oregon Consumer Information Protection Act. |
| Impact on financial, operational, or other activities of the listed entity, quantifiable in monetary terms to the extent possible | As part of the Assurance of Voluntary Compliance, IMS will pay a sum of $225,000 to the Oregon Attorney General's Office, with the full amount due within thirty business days of IMS' execution of the Assurance of Voluntary Compliance and its receipt of payment instructions from the Oregon Attorney General's Office. |
