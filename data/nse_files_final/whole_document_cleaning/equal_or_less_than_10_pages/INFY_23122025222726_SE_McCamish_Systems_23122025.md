# Update on McCamish Cyber Incident

December 23, 2025

In continuation to the statement dated March 14, 2025, Infosys Limited updated that on December 22, 2025, Infosys McCamish Systems ("IMS"), a subsidiary of Infosys BPM Limited, which is a wholly owned subsidiary of Infosys Limited, and the State of South Dakota, United States of America, Division of Insurance entered into a Consent Order.

The South Dakota Division of Insurance expressed concerns that IMS may have violated South Dakota Codified Laws related to notification with respect to a 2023 data breach.

IMS has entered into a Consent Order to resolve this matter without any further action. The Consent Order specifically acknowledges that this is not an admission that IMS violated the Acts listed above.

The details as required under Regulation 30 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015, as amended, are as below:

| Particulars | Details |
|---|---|
| Name of the authority | The South Dakota Division of Insurance |
| Nature and details of the action(s) taken, initiated or order(s) passed | Consent Order to resolve the matter without admission of the existence of the alleged violations. |
| Date of receipt of direction or order, including any ad interim or interim orders, or any other communication from the authority | December 23, 2025, 12:05 a.m. IST |
| Details of the violation(s)/contravention(s) committed or alleged to be committed | The South Dakota Division of Insurance ("DOI") expressed concerns regarding potential violations of South Dakota Codified Laws on the grounds that IMS experienced a data breach in 2023 and failed to respond to the DOI's request for information within 20 days. |
| Impact on financial, operation or other activities of the listed entity, quantifiable in monetary terms to the extent possible | IMS has submitted a payment of $30,000 to the South Dakota Division of Insurance as agreed upon in the Consent Order as a monetary penalty. |
