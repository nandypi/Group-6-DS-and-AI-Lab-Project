Transitioning from AI Experimentation to Realization 
Key Investment Thesis  
• 
Topaz Integration & Developer Scale: Infosys is shifting its AI strategy from 
proof-of-concept to enterprise scale through its Topaz fabric platform, which 
addresses six core AI areas including Strategy & Engineering, Process AI, and 
Legacy Modernization. To ensure rapid implementation, the company has 
aggressively scaled its internal capabilities, deploying GitHub Copilot across 
more than 30,000 developers. 
• 
Deepening Ecosystem Partnerships: To capitalize on the Generative AI 
wave and transition clients from experimentation to production, Infosys has 
forged strategic alliances with leading AI innovators such as Anthropic, OpenAI, 
Google Gemini, NVIDIA, and Microsoft. These partnerships enable the delivery 
of high-value, AI-native solutions to complex, regulated industries. 
• 
Tangible Client Outcomes: The transition to realization is evidenced by highly 
measurable client successes. For instance, Infosys enabled Hertz to 
modernize 3 million lines of Cobol to micro-services 60% faster and at a 60% 
lower cost, while helping Ralph Lauren boost customer engagement by 50% 
through conversational AI. Furthermore, BP has initiated 50 AI-agent projects 
leading to 95% payment accuracy and 50% faster contract validation. 
• 
Record Deal Bookings Fuelling Future AI Deployment: The company 
secured a record USD 14.9 billion in large deal TCV for FY26, representing a 
24% YoY growth, with 55% being net-new deals. This massive backlog—which 
includes an industry-first large AI-first GCC deal for a US regional bank—
provides a fertile foundation to embed AI-led transformations into long-term 
enterprise contracts. 
 
Outlook – Infosys’ outlook is defined by its transition toward AI-led value creation and 
realization-led growth, effectively navigating a volatile macro environment. This evolution is 
underpinned by a confident FY27 revenue guidance band of 1.5%-3.5% CC, which explicitly 
absorbs known structural headwinds such as a 75-100 bps drag from a European 
manufacturing client ramp-down and further onsite-mix reductions. Furthermore, disciplined 
margin execution through Project Maximus (value-based selling and lean automation) allows 
the company to project a stable 20%-22% operating margin for FY27 while absorbing critical 
investments in AI capabilities and talent. As Infosys continues to capture downstream 
enterprise AI spending with its Topaz platform, it remains well-positioned to counterbalance 
deflationary pressures in traditional IT services. 
Valuation – We project Infosys's Revenue / EBITDA / PAT CAGR at 5.5% / 7.7% / 5.3% over 
FY26-28E, navigating near-term onsite-mix shifts and European manufacturing headwinds. 
Supported by the Topaz AI fabric and efficiencies from Project Maximus, we expect the 
company to comfortably sustain its 20%-22% operating margin guidance while funding new AI 
capabilities. We value the stock at 20x FY28E EPS of INR 78.8, which translates to a Target 
Price of INR 1,497 per share, implying a 30% upside from the current market price.

Conference Call Highlights  
 
Demand & Outlook — FY27 Guidance 
 
• 
FY27 revenue guidance 1.5–3.5% CC — narrower 200-bps band (vs 3-point band last year), reflecting better environment visibility. 
Includes Missing Link acquisition (closed this week); excludes Versen JV + Optimum Healthcare. 
• 
FY27 operating margin guidance 20–22% — headwinds from wage hikes, productivity pass-throughs, AI investments; offset by 
Project Maximus. Optimum Healthcare post-closure to dilute margin by ~0.7% on full-year annualised basis. 
• 
Structural headwinds baked into guidance: -75 to -100 bps from one European manufacturing client (ramp-down + conscious walk-
away from non-return-accretive deal); -75 to -100 bps from further onsite-mix reduction (40–50 bps already visible in Q4 exit 
trajectory). Underlying organic growth implied at ~4.25–4.50%. 
 
Vertical & Geography Performance 
 
• 
FY26 vertical growth: Communications, Manufacturing, Europe grew >2x company avg on large-deal ramp-ups. FS +4.4% YoY 
(above avg, AI-led transformation + vendor consolidation). 18 of top-20 FS clients = strategic AI partner. 
• 
Q4 vertical growth: Communications, URS, LS all grew well-above company avg YoY CC. Manufacturing cautious on auto/Europe, 
tariff + Middle-East impact; Retail muted consumer demand; Communications budgets flat to negative. 
• 
URS stand-out: 80% of FY26 URS TCV was net-new — sets up FY27 growth acceleration. Utilities demand structurally higher on 
grid constraints, renewables, data-centre electricity needs. 
 
Large Deals & Bookings 
 
• 
FY26 TCV USD 14.9 bn (+24% YoY) across 96 deals, 55% net-new, 3 mega deals. Q4 TCV USD 3.2 bn across 19 large deals (5 
FS, 5 Mfg, 4 Retail, 2 LS, 2 Comms, 1 EURS). Geo split: 11 Europe, 5 Americas, 3 RoW. 
• 
Large-deal pipeline robust. Included industry-first large AI-first GCC deal for a US regional bank. Net-new % dip vs prior years reflects 
denominator effect, not capability issue — absolute net-new TCV up +25% YoY. 
 
AI Strategy & Partnerships 
 
• 
TOPAS fabric platform (launched at AI Investor Day) addresses six AI service areas: AI Strategy & Engineering, Data, Process, 
Legacy Modernization, Physical AI, Trust. 
• 
Strategic AI partnerships: Anthropic, OpenAI, Google Gemini, NVIDIA, Microsoft, AWS, Google Cloud, Intel. 30,000+ developers 
deployed on GitHub Copilot. 
• 
Marquee AI deployments: Ralph Lauren (+12% revenue / +50% engagement via conversational AI); Hertz (3mn lines Cobol → micro-
services at 60% lower cost, 60% faster); BP (50 AI-agent initiatives — 95% payment accuracy, 50% faster contract validation, +18% 
IT ops efficiency). 
• 
AI services economics: AI projects come in at better pricing and better gross margin than core services, but carry higher cost (premium 
talent). Premium directly tied to being ahead of the curve. 
 
Operational & Capital 
 
• 
Headcount 328,000+ (FY26 exit); voluntary attrition 12.6% (-1.5 ppt YoY); utilisation ex-trainees 83% Q4 / 84.4% FY26. FY27 freshers 
~20,000 (same as FY26). No plan for headcount reduction. 
• 
DSO 78 days (billed + unbilled net of unearned) — lowest in 7 years. Onsite mix 22.8% (-30 bps QoQ); further reduction of 40–50 
bps expected in FY27 exit trajectory. 
• 
FY26 capital return > USD 4 bn; Final dividend INR 25/share — total FY26 DPS INR 48 (+11.6% YoY). ROE 31.6%; adjusted FCF/NI 
106% FY26 (111% Q4). FY27 ETR guided 29–30%. 
 
Key Risks & Research Caveats 
 
• 
AI compression path unquantified — management declined to split gross-growth vs compression; no framework for buyside to model 
deflation-adjusted bookings-to-revenue bridge. 
• 
AI-services revenue not disclosed despite strong qualitative emphasis — contrast vs HCL Tech's USD 155 mn Q4 Advanced-AI 
disclosure. 
• 
European manufacturing client identity, duration, FY28 recovery undisclosed — 75–100 bps FY27 drag but no visibility beyond. 
• 
Wage hike timing for FY27 not decided — introduces margin-phasing uncertainty.
