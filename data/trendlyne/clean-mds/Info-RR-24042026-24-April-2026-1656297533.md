# Infosys — Q4FY26 Result Review

Rating: **HOLD** | TP: ₹1,355 | CMP: ₹1,243 | Potential upside/downside: 9% | Previous Rating: HOLD

Delivering growth with operational discipline and innovation

April 24, 2026

## Summary

Infosys delivered a solid Q4FY26 with revenue growing 4.1% YoY in constant currency, while full-year FY26 revenue crossed USD 20bn with 3.1% CC growth, finishing within the upgraded guidance band. Large deal momentum was exceptional, with full-year deal TCV stood at USD 14.9bn, up 24% YoY, including 96 deals of which 55% were net new, reflecting strong client relevance and competitive positioning. Operating margin for Q4 stood at 20.9%, with full-year adjusted margin remained stable at 21%, as currency and Project Maximus gains are reinvested into AI talent, sales and partnerships. Financial services, communications, manufacturing and Europe were standout performers for the year. Free cash flow remained healthy at USD 3.5bn with conversion well above 100% of net profit. DSO hit a seven-year low of 78 days. For FY27, Infosys guided revenue growth of 1.5% to 3.5% CC with EBIT margin of 20-22%, factoring in AI-led productivity compression, a large European manufacturing client ramp-down worth 75-100bps headwind, on-site mix reduction impact of ~70bps, and wage hike headwinds, offset by strong AI services growth across its Topaz Fabric platform and continued large deal ramp-ups. While maintaining our long-term positive outlook, we maintain our rating to HOLD with a revised TP to Rs 1,355 (16.5x FY28E EPS).

## Key Highlights and Investment Rationale

* **Revenue & Margin:** Management expects 1.5% to 3.5% revenue growth in FY27 with a margin of 20-22%, driven by AI services, cost optimization and large deals.
* **AI related capabilities:** Infosys displayed strong AI capabilities with platforms like Topaz fabric, Cobalt, supporting clients across areas, enhancing automation, modernization, and collaborating with leading AI companies like OpenAI and Google.

## Other Key Highlights

* Infosys had a strong financial performance in the year 2026, with a growth of 3.1% in revenue in constant currency terms, showing steady progress. In the last quarter, Q4, their revenue grew by 4.1% compared to the same period last year, indicating good quarterly growth.
* During the quarter, they signed deals worth USD 3.2bn, demonstrating strong deal making in the last three months. Infosys secured large deals worth USD 14.9bn for the full year, which is 24% more than the previous year, showing their ability to win big contracts. The company experienced strong growth in sectors like financial services, communications, manufacturing, and in Europe during the year.
* The company shared their AI strategy, focusing on six areas such as AI engineering, data, process automation, legacy modernization, physical AI, and trust. They have platforms like Topaz Fabric for AI and Cobalt Platform for Cloud, which help them serve their clients better in AI and cloud services.
* Infosys helped a retail company, Ralph Lauren, create a personalized AI tool that increased their revenue by 12% and customer engagement by 50%. For Hertz, a transport company, Infosys migrated 3 million lines of code to a modern system using AI, which was 60% cheaper and faster than traditional methods.
* They also worked with BP, an energy company, deploying AI agents that improved payment accuracy, sped up contract validation, and increased operational efficiency. The company has partnerships with top AI companies like OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and others, which support their AI development efforts.
* Infosys expects their revenue for the year 2027 to grow by 1.5% to 3.5%, mainly driven by AI services and existing large deals. They plan to keep operating margins between 20% and 22%, focusing on cost control and investing in AI and automation.
* Infosys expects to hire around 20,000 new graduates in the next year and aims to maintain or slightly reduce their total employee number over time. The company is optimistic about future growth, supported by their AI strategy, strong large deals pipeline, and ongoing client demand despite market uncertainties.
