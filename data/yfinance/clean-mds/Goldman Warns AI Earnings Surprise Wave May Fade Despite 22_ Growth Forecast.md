# Goldman Warns AI Earnings Surprise Wave May Fade Despite 22% Growth Forecast

Khac Phu Nguyen
Wed, July 8, 2026 at 9:57 PM GMT+5:30 • 1 min read

GS

This article first appeared on GuruFocus.

Goldman Sachs Group (NYSE:GS), a global investment banking and asset management firm, has cautioned that the AI-fueled earnings surprise cycle that helped power stocks in the last reporting season may be difficult to repeat. Christian Mueller-Glissmann, Goldman's head of asset allocation research, said companies are still likely to beat expectations, but the bar for this earnings season is already high. Goldman's consensus forecast points to 22% second-quarter earnings growth for S&P 500 (SPY) companies, broadly matching estimates compiled by Bloomberg Intelligence.

Mueller-Glissmann emphasized that investors may now pay closer attention to outlooks and management commentary, rather than just headline earnings beats, as they assess whether equities have more room to move higher. He noted that late-cycle earnings revisions can continue for some time, but the large AI capex-linked earnings surprise wave appears to be closer to its end. This suggests the market may require stronger forward-looking signals before pricing in another major rally.

The largest U.S. technology firms are planning to spend as much as $725 billion this year on data centers, specialized chips, and networking equipment, underscoring the scale of the AI investment cycle. Mueller-Glissmann said hyperscalers remain well-positioned because they control significant AI infrastructure, while adding that they should begin focusing more on efficiency and AI monetization. He also said Goldman's view is that the broader structural AI trend remains intact, even as investors may become more selective about which companies can convert AI spending into earnings growth.
