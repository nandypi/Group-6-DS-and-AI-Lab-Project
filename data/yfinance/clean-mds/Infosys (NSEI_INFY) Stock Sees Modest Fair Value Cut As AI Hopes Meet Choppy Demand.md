# Infosys (NSEI:INFY) Stock Sees Modest Fair Value Cut As AI Hopes Meet Choppy Demand

Bailey Pemberton
Fri, July 3, 2026 at 6:47 PM GMT+5:30 • 3 min read

INFY.NS -1.73% INFY -0.84%

Infosys sits at the center of a fresh reset in analyst expectations, with one key model trimming its fair value estimate from ₹1,460.95 to ₹1,395.13, a reduction of about 4.5%. That shift lines up with recent research that mixes optimism about Infosys exposure to AI with caution around growth execution, tech spending trends, and the timing of any benefits from new AI related work. As you read on, you will see how these changing price targets, research notes, and deal headlines can help you track the evolving Infosys investment story.

## What Wall Street Has Been Saying

**Bullish Takeaways**

* JPMorgan keeps an Overweight rating on Infosys, indicating the firm still sees the stock as relatively attractive within India IT services even after revising its price target to US$12.70.
* Across recent notes, including JPMorgan, analysts acknowledge that Infosys is exposed to generative AI trends, which could support longer term deal activity if the company converts interest into concrete projects.

**Bearish Takeaways**

* Wells Fargo initiated Infosys at Equal Weight with a US$11 price target, flagging a "choppy" demand setup and the view that the stock lacks clear near term catalysts. The firm prefers to wait for more evidence before turning more positive.
* JPMorgan describes India IT services demand as uncertain, citing an "unprecedented confluence" of generative AI related cost pressures and geopolitics. The firm notes that tech services budgets are being crowded out by spending on AI tokens and cloud, which can weigh on Infosys growth visibility.

We've flagged 1 risk for Infosys.

## How This Changes the Fair Value For Infosys

* The fair value estimate has been revised from ₹1,460.95 to ₹1,395.13, reflecting a reduction of about 4.5% in the analyst estimate.
* The revenue growth assumption has been adjusted from 4.05% to 3.70%.
* The net profit margin assumption has been updated from 16.22% to 16.29%.
* The future P/E multiple has been changed from 24.1x to 23.1x.
* The discount rate has been updated from 15.15% to 15.20%.
